Real train movements turned into live betting markets

The Platform is a rail-based betting product built around real UK train data. It works in three formats: betting on one long-distance service, betting across the wider network, or betting on live movements at a single busy station.

The three products

One idea, three ways to play.

The product is easiest to understand as three connected formats. Each one uses train movement data, but each creates a different type of betting experience.

01

The Journey

A named train journey from one city to another. Players bet on what happens at checkpoints along the route.

  • Checkpoint stations
  • Delay bands
  • Direction and timing markets

Core live product
The Journey

Betting on one train from start to finish.

The Journey follows a single long-distance service. The player is not betting on a whole sport or a whole station. They are following one train as it moves through a route.

This gives the product a clear story. The train leaves, reaches checkpoints, gains or loses time, and settles markets as it moves.

Player question Will this train be early, on time or late at the next checkpoint?
Possible markets Delay band, arrival time, first checkpoint on time, final station delay.
Best use Longer-form play with a clear route and a story the player can follow.
The Network

An accumulator built from live train events.

The Network takes the familiar betting accumulator format and applies it to rail. Instead of picking football teams, the player picks outcomes from several live train services.

This format is broader than The Journey. It does not rely on one route. It uses a pool of live services and lets the player combine several outcomes into one bet.

Player question Can I pick several train outcomes correctly in one combined bet?
Possible markets Train A late, Train B on time, Train C arrives before a set time.
Best use A familiar betting format that operators and players already understand.
The Station

The strongest live-data product.

The Station is the simplest and most immediate version of The Platform. A busy station is selected. Players bet on what will happen in the next live window.

The screen becomes a live betting board. Trains arrive, pass, stop or use specific platforms. Outcomes settle as the data confirms what happened.

Player question What will happen at this station in the next few minutes?
Possible markets Total trains, stopping vs passing, direction split, platform use, freight appears.
Best use Fast, repeatable rounds with live settlement and high replay value.

Data and settlement

The data makes the product credible.

The Station version uses live rail feeds to predict, display and settle the markets. The aim is to avoid guesswork and settle against verifiable movement data.

Darwin Real-time ETAs, platform assignments, stopping vs passing classification. Updated continuously via the National Rail Push Port feed.
TRUST Authoritative confirmation of actual train movements to the millisecond. Primary settlement source of record.
Train Describer Physical train position tracked through signalling berths. Confirms location independent of schedule data.
Commercial model

Built for operators, not direct to players.

The Platform is best positioned as a B2B game supplied to licensed operators through an aggregator. The operator gets a new live-data betting product. The Platform earns through a revenue share on GGR.

The Station should lead the pitch because it is the clearest, fastest and easiest format to demonstrate. The Journey and The Network sit around it as additional product formats for operators wanting broader coverage.
